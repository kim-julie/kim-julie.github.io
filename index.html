Research interests

Substantive

Demography, population health metrics, and health inequity — with a focus on how these play out across geography, education, race, and ethnicity.

Methods

Bayesian hierarchical modeling, small-area estimation, survey design and analysis, and time-series forecasting.

Currently

2021 — present

PhD candidate at the University of Washington's Institute for Health Metrics and Evaluation (IHME), on the Future Health Scenarios and US Health Disparities team, working with Stein Emil Vollset and Laura Dwyer-Lindgren.

My dissertation develops a Bayesian approach for estimating subnational fertility, mortality, and migration across social and geographic dimensions, combining survey and administrative data. I hold a Certificate in Demographic Methods.
