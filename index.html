Research interests

Substantive

Demography, population forecasting, population health metrics, and health inequity — with a focus on how these play out across geography, education, race, and ethnicity.

Methods

Demographic methods, Bayesian hierarchical modeling, small-area estimation, survey design and analysis, and time-series forecasting.

Research experience

Starting Fall 2026

I'm a statistical demographer specializing in population forecasting and demographic methods. Incoming postdoctoral fellow at Stanford University , following my PhD in Health Metrics Sciences at the University of Washington's Institute for Health Metrics and Evaluation (IHME), where I worked on the Future Health Scenarios and US Health Disparities team with Stein Emil Vollset and Laura Dwyer-Lindgren.

My dissertation developed a Bayesian approach for estimating subnational fertility, mortality, and migration across social and geographic dimensions, combining survey and administrative data. I hold a Certificate in Demographic Methods.
