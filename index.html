Research interests

Research experience

full statement on the Research page

I'm a statistical demographer specializing in population forecasting and demographic methods, completing my PhD in Health Metrics Sciences at the University of Washington's Institute for Health Metrics and Evaluation (IHME), where I worked on the Future Health Scenarios and US Health Disparities team with Stein Emil Vollset and Laura Dwyer-Lindgren.

My dissertation developed a Bayesian approach for estimating subnational fertility, mortality, and migration across social and geographic dimensions, combining survey and administrative data. I hold a Certificate in Demographic Methods.

The two sides of my work inform each other directly: the small-area estimation methods I build to reveal subnational inequities also sharpen the covariates behind IHME's global fertility and migration forecasts — and the forecasts, in turn, show me where finer-grained subnational estimation is needed most.
